## What This Is

This document isn't a contract — it's a map. Marriage doesn't run on autopilot, and the couples who thrive long-term aren't the ones who never have problems; they're the ones who check in regularly, stay curious about each other, and grow intentionally instead of just drifting.

This is our framework for doing that.

## Our Pillars

Before goals, ground yourself in what you both value. Consider discussing and writing down your answers to:

- What does a great marriage look like to each of us?
- What does each of us need to feel loved and supported?
- What are our non-negotiables?
- What are we each willing to work on?

There are no right answers — only honest ones.

## Our Rhythms

Healthy marriages have built-in moments for connection. These don't need to be elaborate — they just need to be consistent.

### Weekly Date (Protected Time)
- Same day each week, no cancellations unless truly unavoidable
- Doesn't need to be expensive — a walk, cooking together, a board game, a movie
- Phones away during this time
- Rotate who plans it

### Monthly "State of Us" Check-In (30 minutes)
A low-stakes monthly conversation about how things are going. Not a conflict session — a calibration. Cover:

1. What's been going well between us this month?
2. Is there anything that's been bothering me that I haven't mentioned?
3. Is there anything I need more of from you right now?
4. What are you excited about or looking forward to?

The goal is to keep small things small by naming them before they grow.

### Annual Relationship Review (1–2 hours, around your anniversary or New Year)
Once a year, do a deeper look:

- Review last year's goals — what did we accomplish? What shifted?
- Set new goals for the coming year
- Revisit your core values and pillars
- Celebrate what's working
- Honestly discuss anything that needs attention

This can be done over a special dinner at home, a weekend trip, or anywhere that feels intentional.

## Navigating Conflict

Every couple argues. The goal isn't to eliminate conflict — it's to fight in a way that brings you closer instead of further apart.

A few agreed-upon rules for hard conversations:

- **No big conversations when hungry, tired, or distracted.** Ask: "Can we talk about this after dinner?" is always okay.
- **Speak for yourself.** "I feel ___" instead of "You always ___."
- **One topic at a time.** Don't drag up old grievances when discussing a current issue.
- **Take breaks when flooded.** If emotions are too high, it's okay to pause — but agree to come back to it within 24 hours.
- **Repair matters more than winning.** After a fight, reconnect. A small gesture goes a long way.

## Growing Individually

A strong marriage is made of two people who are also growing on their own. Support each other's individual interests, friendships, and goals — not just the shared ones.

- Encourage each other to maintain friendships outside the marriage
- Celebrate each other's individual wins
- Give each other space without making it feel like abandonment
- Check in on each other's personal goals, not just household ones
