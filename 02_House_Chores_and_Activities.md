# 🏠 House Chores & Activities
*Reyes-Altuve Household — May 2026*

---

## Our Philosophy

We're not running a hotel — we're running a home. The goal isn't a spotless house 24/7; it's a comfortable, functional space we both feel good in without one person carrying the whole load. This system is built on ownership, clarity, and a shared definition of "done."

---

## Core Principles

1. **Ownership over rotation** — each chore has a permanent owner. Rotation sounds fair but leads to confusion and "I thought it was your turn" arguments.
2. **Agree on "good enough"** — define the standard together so neither person feels like they're constantly redoing the other's work.
3. **Adjust without guilt** — life changes, energy levels change. Review and swap ownership if something isn't working.
4. **Cats are a shared responsibility** — litter, feeding, and play are household duties, not one person's job.

---

## Chore Frequency Tiers

Rather than a daily to-do list for everything, we organize chores by how often they actually need doing:

### Daily
- Wash dishes / load dishwasher
- Clean litter boxes (all four cats — at least once a day)
- Wipe down kitchen counters after cooking
- General tidying (put things back where they belong)
- Feed cats (morning and evening)

### Weekly
- Vacuum and sweep all floors
- Mop kitchen and bathroom floors
- Clean bathrooms (toilet, sink, shower/tub)
- Take out trash and recycling
- Change bed sheets
- Wipe down stovetop and microwave
- Water plants (if any)

### Monthly
- Deep clean refrigerator (remove old food, wipe shelves)
- Clean inside microwave thoroughly
- Wipe down cabinet fronts and light switches
- Organize a drawer, shelf, or cabinet
- Descale coffee maker or kettle
- Brush cats and check for any health concerns

### Quarterly / Seasonally
- Clean windows (inside)
- Vacuum behind and under large furniture
- Wash shower curtain or deep-clean glass doors
- Declutter — donate or toss things not used in 6 months
- Check smoke detector batteries
- Deep clean oven

## The "Good Enough" Standard

For each area, agree on what completion looks like. Some examples:

- **Dishes:** Counter clear, no dishes in the sink before bed.
- **Vacuuming:** Main living areas and bedroom done, cat hair visibly removed from furniture.
- **Bathrooms:** No visible grime on toilet, sink rinsed, mirror wiped.
- **Laundry:** Clean clothes folded and put away (not left in the dryer).
- **Litter boxes:** Scooped at least once daily, fully changed weekly.

The goal is a shared standard — not perfection, not "whatever" either.

## Dealing With Busy Weeks

Some weeks are harder than others. A few ground rules for when life gets busy:

- **Communicate early** — if you know you're going to have a rough week, say so. Your partner can pick up some slack without resentment if they're not surprised by it.
- **Triage** — identify which chores actually matter most that week and let the rest slide guilt-free.
- **Reset on weekends** — use Saturday or Sunday to do a quick reset of the house so the new week starts clean.

---

## Monthly Chore Check-In (10 minutes)

Once a month, spend a few minutes asking each other:

- Is the current ownership split still working?
- Is anything being skipped regularly? Why?
- Do we need to add, remove, or adjust anything?

This prevents small frustrations from becoming big arguments.

---

## Cat Care Specifics 🐱

With four cats, cat care is a significant part of household maintenance. A few things worth standardizing:

- **Litter boxes:** Aim for at least 1 box per cat. Scoop daily; full change weekly. Track which cat uses which box if there are any health concerns.
- **Feeding:** Set fixed times (e.g., 7am and 7pm) so cats aren't pestering you all day. Note any cat that's eating significantly less or more than usual.
- **Vet scheduling:** Keep a shared note with each cat's last checkup date and due dates for vaccinations and treatments.
- **Play time:** 10–15 minutes of active play per day per cat reduces stress and behavioral issues. Rotate toys monthly to keep things interesting.

## Seasonal Household Projects

Some things don't fit into weekly routines but still need to happen. Plan these together at the start of each season:

- **Spring:** Deep clean, declutter, refresh home organization
- **Summer:** Air conditioning maintenance, check fans, outdoor areas
- **Fall:** Check heating system, prepare for colder months
- **Winter:** Holiday decorating and undecorating, cozy-ify the space
