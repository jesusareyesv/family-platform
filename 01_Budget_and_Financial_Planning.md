# 💰 Budget & Financial Planning
*Reyes-Altuve Household — May 2026*

---

## Why This Matters

Money is one of the most common sources of friction in a relationship — not because couples disagree on values, but because they never clearly agreed on a system. This document is our system. It's meant to be simple, flexible, and something we actually use.

---

## Our Core Principles

1. **Transparency over secrecy** — we both know what's coming in and going out.
2. **Autonomy within structure** — we each have personal spending money, no questions asked.
3. **Progress over perfection** — a rough budget we follow is better than a perfect one we ignore.
4. **Review together, decide together** — no major financial decision is made solo.

---

## Account Structure

We use a three-account model to keep things clean:

| Account | Purpose | Who contributes |
|---|---|---|
| **Joint Household Account** | All shared expenses: rent, utilities, groceries, vet bills, subscriptions | Both of us, agreed split |
| **Jesus's Personal Account** | Personal spending, no justification needed | Jesus |
| **Wife's Personal Account** | Personal spending, no justification needed | Wife |

> 💡 The joint account should have a small buffer (1–2 months of expenses) so unexpected costs don't cause stress.

## Budget Categories

### Fixed Costs (same every month)
- Rent / mortgage
- Utilities (electricity, water, internet)
- Insurance (health, car, home/renters)
- Subscriptions (streaming, apps, memberships)
- Loan or credit card minimums

### Variable Necessities (changes month to month)
- Groceries
- Gas / transportation
- Cat supplies (food, litter, vet)
- Household supplies (cleaning, toiletries)

### Discretionary (fun money)
- Dining out / takeout
- Entertainment & activities
- Clothing & personal care
- Gifts

### Savings & Future
- Emergency fund (target: 3–6 months of expenses)
- Vacation / travel fund
- Home improvement fund
- Long-term savings / investments

## The Weekly Check-In (15 minutes, every Sunday)

Pick a consistent time — Sunday evening works well. Sit down together with a coffee or snack and go through:

1. What did we spend this week?
2. Are we on track with the month's budget?
3. Any upcoming expenses to plan for?
4. Anything to adjust?

This keeps surprises away and makes money a regular, low-stakes conversation instead of a crisis topic.

---

## The Quarterly Financial Date (1–2 hours, every 3 months)

Four times a year, do a deeper review:

- Are we hitting our savings goals?
- Any subscriptions or services to cut?
- Did our income or expenses change significantly?
- Are we on track for any big purchases (vacation, new appliance, etc.)?
- Update the budget if needed

Treat this like a real date — put it in the calendar, maybe do it over a nice dinner at home.

---

## Emergency Fund Rule

Before investing or saving for goals, we maintain an emergency fund covering **3–6 months of total household expenses**. This lives in a separate savings account and is only touched for genuine emergencies (job loss, major medical, critical car repair).

Rebuilding the emergency fund always takes priority over discretionary spending if it's ever used.

---

## Decision Rules

- **Under $50:** Either person can spend from personal account freely.
- **$50–$200 from joint account:** Quick heads-up to the other person.
- **Over $200 from joint account:** Brief conversation before purchasing.
- **Major purchases (over $500):** Planned and agreed upon in advance, ideally during a weekly or quarterly check-in.

---

## Tools

Pick one and stick with it — consistency matters more than the tool:

- **YNAB (You Need A Budget)** — best for couples, zero-based budgeting
- **Shared spreadsheet** — simple and fully customizable
- **Copilot / Monarch Money** — automated tracking with shared access

---

## A Note on Cat Expenses 🐱

Four cats means four sets of vet visits, food, litter, and the occasional surprise bill. We treat cat expenses as a **household necessity**, not discretionary spending. Consider setting aside a small monthly amount into a dedicated "cat fund" to cover annual vet checkups and any unexpected health issues without stress.
